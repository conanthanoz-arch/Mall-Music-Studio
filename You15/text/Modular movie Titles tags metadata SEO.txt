Part 2: Automated Live Chat Moderation Plan
A 24/7 stream live chat can quickly fill up with spam links, self-promotion, or toxic messages while you are asleep. To keep the chat friendly and relaxing without needing a team of human moderators, you can use Nightbot or Streamlabs Cloudbot combined with YouTube's built-in chat safety controls.
1. YouTube Studio Built-in Moderation Filters
Configure these settings in your YouTube Live Dashboard under Settings > Community:

	•	Block Words: Add a custom list of blacklisted words, including common self-promotion phrases (e.g., check out my channel, sub back, follow me, soundcloud.com). If someone types these, their message is instantly hidden.
	•	Block Links (ON): Check this box. It prevents users from posting malicious URLs, sketchy links, or unauthorized advertisements in your live feed.
	•	Slow Mode (ON): Set this to 3 to 5 seconds. This prevents bots or angry users from flooding your chat feed with rapid-fire spam, keeping the scroll speed readable and relaxing for your genuine community.
