Modular movie style shifts on 6 hour chunks

Exploring rotating visual styles alongside evolving music subgenres across different 6-hour broadcast blocks is the ultimate way to scale this channel into a massive, highly addictive ambient network. By switching styles every 6 hours, your channel will cater to different online aesthetics—drawing in distinct listener subcultures throughout the day.
Here is the strategic blueprint to structure your multi-style network and programmatically automate the theme swaps.

2. Modulating the Python Code for Theme Swaps
Instead of hardcoding a single background image, you can modify your setup to store your assets in theme folders (e.g., assets/pixel_art/, assets/anime/).
Your master loop can use simple math (current_block = (frame // STR_BLOCK_FRAMES) % 4) to automatically unload the old art style from RAM and load the next theme's assets the exact second a 6-hour block concludes.
3. Cross-Aesthetic Advertiser Appeal
This rotational approach is an incredibly powerful selling point for your advertiser onboarding pitch:

	•	Targeted Placements: Brands can choose which artistic aesthetic aligns best with their product. A retro clothing brand might pay a premium to appear strictly during your 16-Bit Pixel Art block, while an energy drink or tech startup might prefer the Cyberpunk Neon block.
	•	Dynamic Asset Logs: Your existing advertiser_log.csv architecture easily handles this. You simply add a Theme_Block column to the spreadsheet, and your asset loader will pull that specific advertiser's logo only when that matching style block is currently active on the stream.
