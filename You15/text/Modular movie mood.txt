## 2. Automating the Black-Ambient Fade Matrix
To achieve the precise, shifting tides of darkness found in *Disconnecting States*, your script cannot use simple linear audio crossfades. It needs an automated **S-Curve (Sigmoid) Audio Fade** that makes the music swell from the background like an encroaching tide, rather than a predictable, mechanical edit.
Here is how we integrate the complex audio mixing and the custom dark ambient layer automation into the script:
```python
import ffmpeg

def apply_cosmic_ambient_weave(video_in, action_audio, ambient_track, step_index, phase_config):
    """
    Automates the swelling, deep-ambient audio fades using exponential S-curves
    to mimic the dark, shifting textures of Inade and Herbst9.
    """
    video = ffmpeg.input(video_in).video
    action = ffmpeg.input(action_audio).audio
    ambient = ffmpeg.input(ambient_track).audio
    
    duration = phase_config.get("duration", 10.0)
    fade_in_dur = phase_config.get("ambient_fade_in", 3.0)
    fade_out_dur = phase_config.get("ambient_fade_out", 3.0)
    
    # 1. Apply Lynchian/Lovecraftian processing to the ambient track
    # Herbs9/Inade texture: Pitch down heavily (-12 semitones) and slow down to create a vast cavernous feel
    processed_ambient = (
        ambient
        .filter('asetrate', 44100 * 0.5) # Drastically drops pitch and speed simultaneously
        .filter('aresample', 44100)
    )
    
    # 2. Advanced Fade Automation: Creating the wave effect (Inade style)
    # The ambient track swells in, dominates the mid-scene, and decays out into the door transition
    processed_ambient = (
        processed_ambient
        .filter('afade', type='in', start=0, duration=fade_in_dur, curve='quas')
        .filter('afade', type='out', start=duration - fade_out_dur, duration=fade_out_dur, curve='qsin')
    )
    
    # 3. Apply Deutsch Nepal style rhythmic distortion during Feral/Nexus phases
    if phase_config.get("apply_industrial_rot", False):
        # Overdrive the audio channels slightly and apply a low-pass filter to keep it muddy and chest-rumbling
        processed_ambient = (
            processed_ambient
            .filter('acrusher', level_in=1, level_out=0.5, bits=8, mode='log') # Lo-fi analog crunch
            .filter('lowpass', f=180) # Cut out high frequencies, leaving pure suffocating sub-bass
        )
        
    # 4. Master Mix: Combine the sharp local foley (footsteps/doors) with the cosmic void
    mixed_audio = ffmpeg.filter([action, processed_ambient], 'amix', inputs=2, duration='first')
    
    return ffmpeg.output(video, mixed_audio, f"./output_loops/step_{step_index}_sonic_weave.mp4")

```
## 3. Creating the Asset Library (The Low-Cost Recipe)
To generate these exact textures without a studio, use a free digital audio workstation (like Audacity or Reaper) to bake your baseline stems before running them through the pipeline script:
 1. **The Vinterriket Frozen Wind:** Record 30 seconds of open air or a simple white noise generator. Apply a sharp bandpass filter around 400Hz, add a massive reverb (100\% wet, 10-second decay), and pitch it down. This creates a cold, empty drone.
 2. **The Herbst9 Occult Substrate:** Record yourself dragging a heavy metal object (like a frying pan or a wrench) slowly across a rough concrete floor. Slow the track down by 500\%. The concrete friction will turn into an ancient, terrifying ritualistic texture.
 3. **The Deutsch Nepal Pulsing Rot:** Record a single, rhythmic thump (hitting a wooden table with your fist). Loop it every 1.5 seconds. Apply a heavy distortion or "bitcrusher" effect, then cut the high frequencies. It will sound like a massive, distant industrial piston pumping in an underground complex.
By feeding these specific, highly textured dark-ambient stems into the automation array, the video loops will seamlessly shift from a mundane walk through a hallway into a terrifying psychological descent where time, space, and identity are utterly consumed by the void.
