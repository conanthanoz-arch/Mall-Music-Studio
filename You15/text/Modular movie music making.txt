2. Map Out Your Programmatic Song Structure
To keep your generated music from sounding like random, disjointed notes, your Python code needs a structural roadmap for each song loop. Sketch out a standard 3-minute arrangement template in your notes:

	•	Intro (16 bars): Code triggers just the ambient mall texture (mall_day_ambiance.mp3) and a basic, slow-fading electric piano chord progression.
	•	The Verse/Core (32 bars): Code activates your generated drum loop generation sequence (kick, snare, hi-hat) and kicks in the main melodic synth or guitar line.
	•	The Bridge/B-Section (16 bars): Code drops the drum layers completely. It modifies the filter cutoff to make the chords sound muffled, simulating a track playing from a speaker deep inside an empty store.
	•	Outro (16 bars): Code strips away the melody, leaving just the bassline and the mall ambiance fading out to zero.
